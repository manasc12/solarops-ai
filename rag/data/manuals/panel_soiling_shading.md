# Panel Soiling and Shading Guide

## Soiling
Soiling is the accumulation of dust, sand, pollen, bird droppings, or snow on the
PV module surface. It reduces irradiance reaching the cells and lowers output.

Symptoms: gradual decline in output that is NOT explained by weather; uniform or
patchy reduction across an array; deviation between forecast and actual of 5-20%.

Recommended actions:
1. Compare soiling ratio against a clean reference cell if available.
2. Schedule cleaning when soiling losses exceed the cleaning cost threshold.
3. In arid regions (e.g., desert sites) inspect after dust storms.

## Shading
Partial shading from vegetation, structures, or adjacent rows reduces output and
can cause hotspots. Even small shaded areas can disproportionately reduce string output.

Symptoms: time-of-day dependent dips; localized output loss; bypass diode activation.

Recommended actions:
1. Trim vegetation encroaching on the array.
2. Verify inter-row spacing and tracker backtracking configuration.
3. Inspect for new obstructions after site works.

## Distinguishing Soiling/Shading from Equipment Faults
- Soiling: slow, weather-independent decline, inverter status OK.
- Shading: time-dependent, repeatable pattern, inverter status OK.
- Equipment fault: sudden drop, inverter status DEGRADED or FAILURE.
